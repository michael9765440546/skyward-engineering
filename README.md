Skyward Engineering - Airfoil Lift Simulator

Project Overview:
This is a Python-based Airfoil Lift Simulator that calculates the lift generated by an aircraft wing and shows how lift changes with velocity. Enter the wing area, lift coefficient, and airspeed to compute lift force. The program also plots a Lift vs Velocity graph to make it easy to understand how lift increases with speed. This helps visualize real-life wing performance, similar to what aerospace engineers do.

Formula Used:
The program uses the standard lift equation:

Lift = 0.5 × air density × velocity² × wing area × lift coefficient

Where:

Lift = Lift force in Newtons (N)

Air density = 1.225 kg/m³ (sea level)

Velocity = aircraft speed in m/s

Wing area = S, in m²

Lift coefficient = Cl, depends on wing shape and angle of attack

Graph Explanation:
The graph shows how lift force changes as the aircraft’s speed increases.

On the X-axis, we have velocity in meters per second (m/s), ranging from 0 to 20 m/s in this example.

On the Y-axis, we have lift force in Newtons (N).

From the graph, you can clearly see that even at low speeds, the lift starts from zero and increases rapidly as velocity increases, because lift is proportional to the square of the speed. This means that small increases in velocity produce larger increases in lift.

For example, with a wing area of 50 m² and a lift coefficient of 0.8, the lift at 80 m/s reaches 156,800 N. The graph makes it easy to visualize this relationship and understand why aircraft need to reach a certain speed before they can take off: at low speeds, lift is small, but as the plane accelerates, lift builds up quickly.

This visualization helps anyone studying or designing wings to see how changes in speed affect lift, and why engineers carefully consider wing area, shape, and speed for safe and efficient flight.
